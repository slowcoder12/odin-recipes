Mutton Curry

Description

Mutton curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish that is popular in many cuisines around the world. It is typically made using tender pieces of lamb or mutton that are cooked in a fragrant sauce made with a variety of spices and herbs. Some of the common spices used in mutton curry include cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, which is a blend of spices commonly used in Indian cooking. Other ingredients may include garlic, ginger, onions, tomatoes, and yogurt, which are used to add flavor and texture to the sauce.

To prepare mutton curry, the meat is usually marinated in a mixture of spices and yogurt for several hours or overnight, which helps to tenderize the meat and infuse it with flavor. The meat is then cooked in a sauce made with onions, tomatoes, and other ingredients until it is tender and fully cooked. Mutton curry can be served with rice, naan bread, or other types of bread, and it is a delicious and satisfying dish that is enjoyed by many people around the world.

Ingredients

  • Lamb or mutton
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Yogurt
  • Spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee
  • Water or broth

Steps

  1. Clean and cut the lamb or mutton into bite-sized pieces. Marinate the meat in a mixture of yogurt and spices for at least 2-3 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add sliced onions and sauté until they become golden brown.
  3. Add minced ginger and garlic to the pot and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Add chopped tomatoes, curry leaves, and spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder to the pot. Stir and cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes are soft and the spices are fragrant.
  4. Add the marinated meat to the pot and stir to coat it with the spice mixture. Pour in enough water or broth to cover the meat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover the pot and cook for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender and fully cooked.
  5. Remove the lid from the pot and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes to thicken the sauce. Adjust the seasoning as necessary and serve hot with rice, naan bread, or other types of bread.
